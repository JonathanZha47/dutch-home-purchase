# Motivation Letter - Teakhout 29, Zaandam

Dear Seller,

I am very happy to submit my offer for Teakhout 29.

This apartment genuinely feels like a place where I could make a home. I really like the light, the layout, the location near the station and city centre, and the comfort of living on the seventh floor in a modern building. The storage space and the possibility of a parking space are also very practical for me.

A close friend of mine already lives in the same apartment complex. Being able to become his neighbour would mean a great deal to me, and it would make this home feel even more special from the start.

This is the only property I am currently bidding on. I have taken the time to carefully review the information and I am serious about moving forward. I would be very grateful for the opportunity to make Teakhout 29 my home and to become part of the community.

I would also like to clarify my earlier indication of EUR 290,000. At that stage, I had not yet fully reviewed the VvE documentation. After reading the minutes of the VvE meeting of 21 May, I saw that there is an unresolved maintenance programme concerning the building envelope, windows, drainage and leakage issues. The minutes refer to a targeted programme estimated at at least EUR 1.2 to 1.4 million, while the final scope, allocation between owners, use of reserves and any possible recovery from third parties are still uncertain.

This does not reduce my enthusiasm for the apartment. However, as a first-time buyer, I need to take this financial uncertainty into account responsibly. That is why I cannot simply maintain the earlier EUR 290,000 indication without clear protection regarding existing or approved VvE special assessments.

My offer reflects both how much I like the home and the need to make a careful, sustainable decision. I hope that the clarity and seriousness of my offer will be appreciated.

Kind regards,  
Yiwei Zha
